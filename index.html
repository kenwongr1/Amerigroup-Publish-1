Global Energy Transition

Leading the Global Energy Transition with Scalable, Science-Driven Innovation

Amerigroup International stands at the forefront of the clean energy revolution, delivering unparalleled expertise and turnkey solutions in hydrogen production and dispensing, methanol fuel cell power generation and range extenders, renewable energy generation with solar, wind turbines, and innovative water solar solutions, liquefied natural gas (LNG) infrastructure — including onshore regasification terminals, offshore Floating Storage and Regasification Units (FSRU), and international LNG supply logistics — as well as power grid modernization and power plant integration with new energy technologies around the globe.

As the urgency of the climate crisis becomes clearer, we support the development of technology innovations, emissions regulations, and policy pathways designed to enable a net-zero emissions future by 2050. By adhering to established frameworks and collaborative strategies, we aim to contribute to lasting solutions that drive progress in emissions reductions and clean energy transitions.

Our Commitment To Progress

Leading the Way Through Innovative Policies and Sustainable Practices

It's alarming to realize that over 70% of global warming emissions come from the energy sector. Annually, nearly 40 billion tons of carbon dioxide are released into the atmosphere by our global energy systems, driving us ever closer to critical climate thresholds. As economies around the world continue to grow and energy demands surge, this figure is expected to rise — unless we act decisively to transform how we generate and consume energy.

Our Strategy
Advancing Impactful Solutions

As an organization driven by data and results, we focus on a singular goal: reducing carbon emissions. This immense task demands innovation and systemic change across multiple fronts. Our approach centers on four critical areas:

  • Shift the narrative to emphasize the scale of the challenge and the comprehensive nature of the solutions required.
  • Transform technology by providing a diverse array of affordable, carbon-free energy solutions such as advanced renewables, zero-carbon fuels, carbon capture, next-generation nuclear power, and geothermal energy.
  • Evolve business models to incorporate scalable, modular energy systems that can be rapidly deployed anywhere.
  • Influence policy to foster the development, demonstration, and widespread adoption of technologies and systems necessary to reach net-zero emissions by midcentury.
